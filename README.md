## Things you should know:
* The Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) job market in India is oversaturated for entrylevel, average-skilled developers but has a severe talent shortage for specialists. India produces 15 lakhs engineering graduates per year, of which 40% is CSE degree holder. Many are unemployable as they do not have enough skills.
* Unless you stand out or graduate from IITs or NITs or Tier 1 college or at least tier 2 college, you will find it hard to get a good job because of competition, AI rapidly developing being able to do simple python programming.
* If you select CSE for ‘job safety’ or ‘20+lpa’ rather than pure interest in computer science, I am sorry to pop your dream but those placements are given on average to exceptional graduates or IITian. Job safety and 20+lpa are not bad reasons but you should not expect it to be given out to everyone, you must work hard for it.
* If you think ’I will just learn what college teaches me’, I am sorry but please choose mechanical or civil engineering. In many state colleges, including some deemed university the curriculum is outdated. If you wish to exceptional, please self-learn.
* The computer science sector is ever-growing, ever-changing field, what is relevant in 2026 will NOT be in 2030, if you want a job where you do not want to learn after college, it is not in IT field.
* The CSE rewards high-Diligence + high-Patience, if you cannot stare at code for thirty not knowing what causes an error, CSE may not for you. But do not be afraid, there is Highaffiliation + High-results jobs and courses out there, pursue them.
